Program Renewal

Comprehension Layer Design

What I did

  • Identified key lexical and stylistic features of each literary text to establish a language baseline
  • Mapped vocabulary demands across materials to match user level and prevent excessive reading difficulty
  • Improved readability by adjusting line length, contrast, and typographic clarity to manage lexical complexity
  • Revised instructional and assessment language to maintain consistency across quizzes, tests, and workbooks

Why it matters

The project operated within a learning context, where workbooks served as a medium for helping users understand literary texts. In this environment, learners rely on consistent linguistic cues to interpret the literary content. Inconsistent or incompatible language use interferes with comprehension, increases cognitive load, and diminishes users' ability to form a clear understanding of the literary work.

Impact

Following the renewal, educators reported a more coherent lesson structure and questions closely aligned with the literary content.

Projects

Language Quality Evaluation

Overview

Participated in two AI language evaluation projects conducted in different institutional settings: one government-funded research project and one commercial AI system. Both aimed to improve the reliability of AI-based English speaking assessment tools.

Workflow & Methodology

Despite differences in target users, both projects followed a shared multi-stage workflow:

  • Pilot phase for initial guideline testing
  • Calibration phase to align linguistic criteria among annotators
  • Main annotation during active model training
  • Iterative feedback cycles to evaluate and stabilize model outputs
  • Final-stage validation

Value

Contributed to stable and interpretable evaluation workflows through consisten linguistic judgment and reviewer alignment.
