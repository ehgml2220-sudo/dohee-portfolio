Program Renewal

Comprehension Layer Design

What I did

  • Identified key lexical and stylistic features of each literary text to establish a language baseline
  • Mapped vocabulary demands across materials to match user level and prevent excessive reading difficulty
  • Improved readability by adjusting line length, contrast, and typographic clarity to manage lexical complexity
  • Revised instructional and assessment language to maintain consistency across quizzes, tests, and workbooks

Why it matters

The project operated within a learning context, where workbooks served as a medium for helping users understand literary texts. In this environment, learners rely on consistent linguistic cues to interpret the literary content. Inconsistent or incompatible language use interferes with comprehension, increasing cognitive load and diminishing users' ability to form a clear understanding of the literary work.

Impact

Following the renewal, educators reported a more coherent lesson structure and questions closely aligned with the literary content.
